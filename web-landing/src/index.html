AgBase for Farmers

AgBase provides a way to collect, store and analyze farm data from things like weighbridges, pasture meters moisture sensors as well as animal health data. You own the data, and can delete it, share it, or export it at any time. You can view reports on this data data to track key performance indicators for your herd and farm over time. You can even receive automatic alerts on your smartphone for triggers such as changes in animal condition.

AgBase for Researchers

Connect your agritech sensors to the cloud with our easy to use APIs. Use AgBase APIs to mine, analyze and visualize your data and schedule automatic actions based on your analysis.

About AgBase

Platform

AgBase is an open-source platform for managing Agritech data. AgBase enables an ecosystem of data-providers who develop innovative sensors, and analytics services who provide services based on the integration and analysis of data:

Data-providers
Agbase enables sensor developers to focus on innovative sensor development. It enables the collection and management of data. e.g. pasture meters, moisture sensors or weigh bridges. AgBase APIs allow for measurements with quantified uncertainty, enabling smarter analysis.
Analytics Services
Developers of software or services based on the analysis of AgBase data. e.g. Lameness categorisation tools, fertiliser weight prediction and automation suppliers. AgBase allows on-farm decisions to be based on these analytics; for example, auto-drafting, farmer alerts by smartphone.
Smart-Devices
AgBase has developed sample apps showing how smart devices can upload data. These include simple pasture-meter, and condition scoring apps.

Service

The AgBase platform is operated by 4i Systems Ltd, and the changes to the AgBase codebase are managed by the AgBase consortium. Access to AgBase, and the AgBase API is free for farmers and light-users, however heavy users of the API will require an AgBase account. Please contact us to discuss your usage requirements.

Lite users
Users creating less than 10 000 API calls per day. Users of the AgBase webpage.
Heavy users
Users creating more than 10 000 API calls per day. These users will require a AgBase analytics account.
